## 3. Why It Needs a Rebuild (The Problems)

- **It looks dated.** Default 2017 WordPress theme. No brand identity, no atmosphere, nothing that matches the mystical work he does.
- **No way to book or pay.** A visitor who's ready to buy has to fill out a generic form and wait. That's where sales leak out.
- **The blog is a graveyard.** An 8-year-old last post signals "inactive" or "out of business." Worse than having no blog.
- **YouTube is invisible.** He's making video content and the site doesn't show it anywhere. Huge missed discovery + trust builder.
- **No email capture.** No newsletter, no lead magnet, no way to build an audience he owns.
- **Weak SEO and mobile experience.** Thin meta descriptions ("This is the home page's excerpt"), no structured content for search.
- **"Entertainment purposes only" is doing him no favors** the way it's placed. (Keep it for legal cover, but move it to the footer — more on this below.)

## 4. Recommended Site Structure

A tight, modern one-to-few-page flow. Built as a "forest trail" — each section is a stop along the path.

**Primary pages:**

1. **Home / The Trailhead** — atmospheric hero, one-line promise, top testimonial, clear "Book a Reading" button, teaser links into everything else.
2. **Readings & Services** — the tiers, cleaned up, each with a clear "what this is for" and a book button.
3. **About Branden** — the origin story, 30-year credibility, the Ginger Unicorn persona, a real photo.
4. **Watch (YouTube)** — embedded latest videos + subscribe call to action. Auto-pulls his newest uploads.
5. **Potions & Offerings** — candles, incense, custom work. Set up so he can actually sell/ship.
6. **Book / Contact** — real scheduling + a warmer contact form.

**Optional / phase 2:**

- **Journal (blog reboot)** — only if he'll commit to posting. Otherwise fold his best existing posts into the About/Readings pages as "musings" and skip a live blog.
- **FAQ** — handles "how does a virtual reading work," "what do I need to prepare," "do you read remotely," etc. Cuts down repeat questions and builds trust.
- **Testimonials wall** — dedicated social-proof page as he collects more.

## 8. The Design Direction: Dark Whimsical Faery Trail

Your instinct is strong and very buildable. Here's how I'd execute it.

**The concept: a walk down the trail.**
The site behaves like moving down a tranquil forest path at dusk. As you scroll, you move deeper into the woods. Each section is a clearing along the trail — the trailhead (home), the reading grove (services), the storyteller's hollow (about), and so on.

**Palette:**

- Deep forest greens, near-black charcoal, and midnight teal as the base.
- Muted moss, fern, and mushroom tones as mid-values.
- Bioluminescent accents: soft glowing amber, moonlit lavender, faery-light gold, wisp blue. These are the "magic" — used on buttons, links, and glowing highlights.

**Motifs:**

- Vines and creeping foliage framing sections.
- Mushrooms (amanita, glowing caps), ferns, twisting tree silhouettes.
- Floating dust/spores, fireflies, and drifting light particles (subtle animation).
- Hand-drawn or storybook-illustrated feel over photographic.

**Motion (subtle, not busy):**

- Gentle parallax so foreground vines/plants drift as you scroll — reinforces "walking the path."
- Soft glowing hover states on buttons (like catching a faery light).
- Fireflies or spores drifting slowly in the background.
- Respect reduced-motion settings for accessibility.

**Typography:**

- A characterful display font for headings (storybook / slightly mystical, still legible).
- A clean, readable serif or humanist sans for body.

**Feel:** tranquil and inviting, not spooky or Halloween-y. Enchanted forest at twilight, not a haunted house. Mysterious but safe — exactly the emotional promise of a good reading.

## 9. How to Build & Stage It (Tech Plan)

**Recommendation: build a static site, host the staging version on GitHub Pages, then point his domain at the finished version.**

Why static (plain HTML/CSS/JS) over rebuilding in WordPress:

- Loads fast, looks fully custom, cheap/free to host, and easy to stage on the `brandentarot` GitHub repo you created.
- Full control over the faery-trail visuals and animation. WordPress themes would fight us on that.
- Branden gets a live preview URL to react to before anything touches the real site.

**Staging flow:**

1. Build the site in the `brandentarot` repo → auto-publishes to `https://rwolfe1979.github.io/brandentarot/` via GitHub Pages.
2. Branden reviews the live staging link, gives feedback, we iterate.
3. When approved, point `brandentarot.com` at it (or hand him the files if he prefers to keep WordPress hosting).

**Booking/payment — pick one (ranked):**

1. **Square Appointments or Acuity/Calendly + Stripe** — real scheduling, takes payment, syncs a calendar. Best experience, small monthly cost. **Recommended.**
2. **Stripe Payment Links / PayPal buttons** — no scheduling, but people can pay per service instantly, then coordinate timing by message. Free-ish, fast to set up. Good middle ground.
3. **Keep it manual** — a nice booking request form that emails him. Cheapest, but slowest and leaks sales. Fallback only.

We can embed whichever he picks into the static site cleanly.
